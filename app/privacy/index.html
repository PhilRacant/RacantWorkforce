Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Racant Workforce collects, uses, and protects your personal information. We are committed to handling your data with care, transparency, and respect.

Who We Are

Racant Workforce is a trading name of Racant Business Services Ltd, registered in England & Wales. We provide care, catering, driving, and staffing services within our local community.

The Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact details — name, address, phone number, email.
  • Service information — details relating to the services you request from us.
  • Application information — if you apply for work with us, we may collect employment history and relevant documents.
  • Website enquiries — information you submit through our contact forms.

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide the services you request.
  • Respond to enquiries and communicate with you.
  • Manage staffing, scheduling, and service delivery.
  • Process job applications and recruitment.
  • Meet our legal and regulatory obligations.

Our Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data under the following lawful bases:

  • Contract — to deliver services you have requested.
  • Consent — when you choose to provide information through our website.
  • Legal obligation — where regulations require us to keep certain records.
  • Legitimate interests — to operate and improve our services responsibly.

How We Store and Protect Your Data

We store personal information securely and take reasonable steps to prevent loss, misuse, or unauthorised access. Only staff who need the information to perform their duties can access it.

How Long We Keep Your Information

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary to provide our services, meet legal requirements, or resolve any issues. After this, information is securely deleted or anonymised.

Sharing Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share data with:

  • Regulators or authorities when legally required.
  • Trusted partners who help us deliver our services (for example, IT or communication providers).

Any partners we work with must keep your information safe and use it only for the agreed purpose.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information.
  • Ask for your data to be deleted when appropriate.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.
  • Object to certain types of processing.
